Conceptual Case Study

A gamified approach
to digital detox

Category : UX/ UI Design

This self-initiated project addresses the issue of smartphone addiction through a gamified experience. By incorporating the fun of raising a virtual cat, the goal was to create an engaging experience that naturally encourages users to take breaks from their digital devices, focusing on “enjoyment” rather than “restriction.”

My role
UX Designer / Researcher

×

Overview

CONTEXT

Daily Smartphone Overuse

I observed how frequent smartphone use affects people’s routines and productivity. This inspired us to rethink smartphone habits in a playful, non-restrictive way.

GOAL

Fun-Driven Habit Change

Instead of forced restrictions, our aim was to design an enjoyable system that helps users naturally step away from their devices by rewarding them through interactive cat-raising gameplay.

TARGET

Everyday Users Wanting Healthier Habits

The project focuses on ordinary smartphone users, including those seeking to reduce screen time in a lighthearted and sustainable way.

Design Process

Hypothesis

Before conducting detailed research, I formulated the following hypotheses based on my previous experience in Japan.

  • • Difficulties for wheelchair users
  • • Lack of clear announcements
  • • Crowded buses making it hard to get on and off

Research

Contextual Inquiry

Before conducting detailed research, I formulated the following hypotheses based on my previous experience in Japan.

Conducted on May 27, 2025

I focused on following key aspects:

• Waiting time at the bus stop
• Boarding behavior
• Passenger activities during the ride
• Actions taken when getting off the bus

Key insights include from my observation

• Buses often do not arrive according to the scheduled timetable.
• The Compass Card tap process causes slight delays.
• Very few passengers use cash for fare payment.
• The LED displays inside the bus are hard to read.
• Announcements are difficult to hear clearly.
• During crowded times, passengers have difficulty reaching the stop buttons.

Stakeholder Mapping

When exploring challenges and potential solutions, it is essential to understand the perspectives and levels of interest of all stakeholders. To support this, I created the stakeholder map (Power-Interest Matrix) shown below, classifying key stakeholders based on their level of power and interest.

Our goal was to design a user-centered solution that makes riding the bus easier for all. We prototyped mobile and in-bus tools to support various user needs.

Solution (1/2)

Wearable Device

Proposed a smart app to support each step of the bus journey.

Why a wearable device?

• Usable even when both hands are full
• Compact and easy to carry
• Intuitive for anyone to operate
• Delivers a smart, stress-free experience
