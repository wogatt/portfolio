Conceptual Case Study

Designing a Seamless Bus Experience

Category : UX/ UI Design

This conceptual project explores how user-centered design can improve public bus experiences. Through contextual research, I identified key accessibility challenges and designed solutions that enable a smoother, more inclusive journey for all riders — including tourists, seniors, and people with disabilities.

My role
UX Designer / Researcher

Overview

CONTEXT

Improving Accessibility in Public Transit

Many bus systems lack inclusivity. In Vancouver, we observed issues like unclear signage and announcements. This led to a redesign focused on improving accessibility for diverse users.

GOAL

A More Inclusive & Seamless Bus Journey

Our goal was to design a user-centered solution that makes riding the bus easier for all. We prototyped mobile and in-bus tools to support various user needs.

TARGET

Diverse Transit Riders

The design focuses on older adults, international visitors, and users with impairments. By testing with real people, we aimed to make the system intuitive and inclusive.

Research

Contextual Inquiry

Before conducting detailed research, I formulated the following hypotheses based on my previous experience in Japan.

Conducted on May 27, 2025

I focused on following key aspects:

• Waiting time at the bus stop
• Boarding behavior
• Passenger activities during the ride
• Actions taken when getting off the bus

Key insights include from my observation

• Buses often do not arrive according to the scheduled timetable.
• The Compass Card tap process causes slight delays.
• Very few passengers use cash for fare payment.
• The LED displays inside the bus are hard to read.
• Announcements are difficult to hear clearly.
• During crowded times, passengers have difficulty reaching the stop buttons.

Solution (1/2)

Wearable Device

Proposed a smart app to support each step of the bus journey.

Why a wearable device?

• Usable even when both hands are full
• Compact and easy to carry
• Intuitive for anyone to operate
